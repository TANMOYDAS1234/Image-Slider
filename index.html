Botanical Gardens
Spread over 270 acres, the Acharya Jagadish Chandra Bose Indian Botanic Garden is a green oasis in the heart of Kolkata. Home to a diverse range of plant species, including the iconic Great Banyan Tree, it's a peaceful retreat away from the city's hustle and bustle.
Howrah Bridge
One of Kolkata's most recognizable landmarks, the Howrah Bridge is a stunning example of engineering marvel. Lit up beautifully at night, it offers breathtaking views of the Hooghly River and the bustling activities along its banks.
Princep Ghat
Situated along the banks of the Hooghly River, Princep Ghat is a picturesque spot where people come to unwind, take boat rides, or simply admire the stunning sunset views. The surrounding colonial-era architecture adds to its charm.
Victoria Memorial
This iconic marble building is a symbol of Kolkata's colonial past. Surrounded by sprawling gardens, it's not only a museum but also a popular spot for locals and tourists alike to relax and enjoy the serene ambiance.
Maidan
The Maidan, often referred to as the "lungs of Kolkata," is a vast urban park in the heart of the city. Covering over 1,000 acres, it's one of the largest urban parks in Asia and serves as a recreational hub for locals and tourists alike
Indian Museum
Established in 1814, the Indian Museum is the oldest and largest museum in India. With its vast collection of artifacts, sculptures, and artworks, it offers a glimpse into the rich cultural heritage of the country. The museum building itself is a splendid example of architectural beauty.
Dakshineswar Kali Temple
Located on the eastern banks of the Hooghly River, this Hindu temple dedicated to Goddess Kali is not only a place of worship but also an architectural masterpiece. The serene surroundings and the riverfront make it a beautiful destination for spiritual seekers and tourists alike.
